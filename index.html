It Ends With Us

It Ends With Us" is a romance novel by Colleen Hoover, first published in 2016. The story follows Lily Bloom, a young woman who moves to Boston to start her own floral business. She becomes involved with Ryle Kincaid, a neurosurgeon with a commitment-averse attitude, while grappling with memories of her first love, Atlas Corrigan. As Lily's relationship with Ryle deepens, she confronts painful truths about her past and present, particularly concerning domestic abuse, leading her to make difficult decisions to break the cycle of violence.

The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo

The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo" is a historical fiction novel by Taylor Jenkins Reid, published in 2017. The story centers on reclusive Hollywood icon Evelyn Hugo, who, at age 79, chooses unknown journalist Monique Grant to write her biography. As Evelyn recounts her rise to fame and her seven marriages, she reveals deep secrets and a forbidden love that defined her life. Through their collaboration, Monique uncovers unexpected connections between her own life and Evelyn's tumultuous journey. It was nominated for a Goodreads Choice Award for Best Historical Fiction in 2017.

Verity

Verity" is a psychological thriller novel by Colleen Hoover, first published in 2018. The story follows Lowen Ashleigh, a struggling writer who is hired to complete the unfinished works of bestselling author Verity Crawford after Verity's debilitating accident. As Lowen delves into Verity's notes, she discovers a hidden autobiography that reveals dark and disturbing secrets about Verity's life, including her relationships and the tragic deaths of her children. The novel explores themes of deceit, obsession, and the blurred lines between fiction and reality, leading readers through a series of shocking twists and moral dilemmas.

Ugly Love

Ugly Love" is a contemporary romance novel by Colleen Hoover, first published in 2014. The story follows Tate Collins, a nursing student, who moves in with her brother and meets Miles Archer, an airline pilot with a troubled past. They enter into a complex, no-strings-attached arrangement, guided by two rules: never ask about the past and don't expect a future. As their physical relationship deepens, Tate finds herself struggling with her growing feelings for Miles, who remains emotionally closed off due to past traumas. The novel explores themes of love, heartbreak, and the challenges of overcoming personal demons to embrace a hopeful future.

November 9

November 9" is a contemporary romance novel by Colleen Hoover, first published in 2015. The story follows Fallon, a young woman scarred both physically and emotionally from a fire, who meets Ben, an aspiring novelist, the day before her planned cross-country move. Their immediate connection leads them to spend Fallon's last day in Los Angeles together, and they agree to meet on the same date—November 9th—every year, without any other contact in between. As years pass, their annual meetings become increasingly significant, intertwining their lives in unexpected ways and challenging their understanding of love and fate.

The Love Hypothesis

The Love Hypothesis" is a contemporary romance novel by Ali Hazelwood, published in 2021. The story follows Olive Smith, a third-year Ph.D. candidate, who impulsively kisses Dr. Adam Carlsen, a young professor with a reputation for being unapproachable, to convince her best friend that she's moved on from a past relationship. Surprisingly, Adam agrees to continue the charade, and they enter into a fake dating arrangement. As their pretend relationship unfolds, Olive and Adam navigate the complexities of academia, personal insecurities, and unexpected feelings, leading them to question the boundaries between pretense and reality.

The Silent Patient

The Silent Patient" is a psychological thriller novel by Alex Michaelides, published in 2019. The story centers on Alicia Berenson, a renowned painter who becomes mute after being accused of murdering her husband, Gabriel. As Theo delves deeper into Alicia's past, he uncovers unexpected revelations that blur the lines between patient and therapist, leading to a shocking conclusion.

Atomic Habits

Atomic Habits: An Easy & Proven Way to Build Good Habits & Break Bad Ones" is a self-help book by James Clear, first published in 2018. The book delves into the science of habit formation, emphasizing how tiny changes can lead to remarkable results over time. Clear introduces the Four Laws of Behavior Change—Make it Obvious, Make it Attractive, Make it Easy, and Make it Satisfying—as a framework for developing good habits and eliminating bad ones. Through practical strategies and real-life examples, the book illustrates how consistent, incremental improvements can compound to produce significant personal and professional growth.

The narrative explores their deepening bond, from friendship to a profound romantic relationship, set against the backdrop of ancient Greek mythology and the looming conflict of the Trojan War. Miller's lyrical prose and fresh perspective on a classic tale have garnered widespread acclaim.

The Midnight Library" is a novel by Matt Haig that explores the choices that make up a life well-lived. The story follows Nora Seed, a woman who, after a series of personal failures, finds herself in a mystical library between life and death. Each book in the library offers her a chance to live a different version of her life, exploring the various paths she could have taken. Through these journeys, Nora gains profound insights into regret, hope, and the power of choice.
